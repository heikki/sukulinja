Tree view — terminology & visualization

Names for every piece of the hourglass-chart layout algorithm. Two canonical examples below are referenced throughout; each term highlights its piece in orange.

Complex case — multi-marriage and half-sibling

Father has had two marriages. Each marriage is a separate Family, and the Families are rendered side-by-side in chronological order — earliest on the left, latest on the right. Mother (1st marriage) is the Primary spouse; she sits adjacent to Father per the Husband-left convention. Step-mother (2nd marriage) fans further right. Focus + Sibling come from the first marriage; Half-siblings come from the second and hang from Step-mother's box bottom (non-Primary Child anchor), with their own bar + legs.

Big picture

Hourglass chart

The whole thing: focus + ancestors above + descendants below.

The overall layout produced by the tree view. A Focus person sits in the middle row; their Ancestor stack fans up, their Descendant stack fans down. The name is historical — multi-spouse fans and lateral relatives can widen any row, so the chart is not literally hourglass-shaped.

Avoid: bowtie chart, kinship chart, pedigree chart.

Focus

The single person the chart is built around.

Ancestors expand upward from the Focus; descendants downward. The Focus's box is rendered with the accent stroke.

Avoid: proband, ego, root, center person.

The three slabs

Focus row

Everything at Focus's Generation (Y = 0).

Contains Focus's own sibship — Focus, every full Sibling, and every Spouse of Focus joined by Ties — plus any Half-sibling sibships beside it (each hanging from the non-Primary Child anchor of a different parent-Couple at the generation above — directly under the step-parent). Primary spouse sits adjacent to Focus per the Husband-left convention.

Avoid: center row, sibship row, generation row.

Ancestor stack

Everything above the Focus row.

At depth 1 (Focus's parents): the bloodline pair, their non-Primary spouses (forming Couples), their full siblings (Aunts/Uncles), and the children of any non-bloodline Couples — Focus's Half-siblings at Focus row. At depth ≥ 2: only the bloodline pair; step-spouses, great-aunts/uncles, and half-aunts/uncles are not rendered. Lateral context is concentrated entirely at depth 1; depth ≥ 2 is a pure bloodline pyramid. Bounded by Generation limit.

Avoid: ascendancy, pedigree, ancestor tree (that's a recursive unit, not the slab).

Descendant stack

Everything below the Focus row.

At each generation down to the Generation limit: the bloodline Descendants (children, grandchildren, …) and each of their spouses (forming Couples). Children of one Family hang from the corresponding Couple's Child anchor.

Avoid: descendancy, descendant tree (that's a recursive unit).

Relatives

Sibling

Another child in the same Family as some reference person.

Focus's siblings are the other children of the Family Focus is a child in. An Ancestor's siblings are Focus's Aunts/Uncles at that ancestor's generation. Rendered in birth order. Children of one Family are always full siblings — Half-siblings arise across different Families.

Half-sibling

Shares exactly one parent with Focus.

Arises when a parent of Focus has children in a Family other than the one Focus is a child in. Rendered at Focus's Generation, in a separate sibship hanging from the corresponding parent-Couple's non-Primary Child anchor — directly under the non-shared parent (the step-parent), not from the long Tie midpoint.

Spouse

A partner in a Family with a rendered person.

Rendered (with a Tie, forming a Couple) when their partner is Focus, a bloodline Ancestor, or a bloodline Descendant. Spouses of lateral relatives — full Siblings, Half-siblings, Aunts/Uncles — are not rendered. When the partner has multiple spouses, each marriage is its own Family; the Families render side-by-side in chronological order, with the Primary spouse adjacent.

Avoid: partner (this codebase models marriage only).

Aunt/Uncle

A full Sibling of Focus's Father or Mother.

Rendered alongside the bloodline parent in the depth-1 childhood FB sibship, hanging from the grandparent-Couple's Tie. Only Focus's own aunts/uncles render — siblings of deeper bloodline ancestors (great-aunts/uncles and beyond) are not rendered.

Avoid: treating aunts/uncles as ancestors — they're collaterals, not bloodline.

Ancestor

A blood ascendant of Focus — parent, grandparent, etc.

Each ancestor has two bloodline parents above (paternal and maternal), and the chart recurses upward through them.

Layout rules

Husband-left convention

Husband always on the left, wife on the right.

A global layout rule applied whenever a husband + wife appear in the same row — ancestor couples, focus-row spouses, descendant-row spouses. When a person has multiple spouses, they appear once at one end of the row (left if husband, right if wife); their marriages fan outward in chronological order, with the Primary spouse adjacent. Stabilizes the orientation of every Tie so they all read "husband-side → wife-side".

Primary spouse

The spouse rendered adjacent to its partner; others fan outward.

When a person has more than one spouse, each marriage is its own Family. The Families render side-by-side, with the Primary spouse adjacent and the rest fanning outward in chronological order. Which marriage is Primary depends on context: for a bloodline Ancestor it's the bloodline partner; for Focus and Descendants it's the most recent marriage. Pure layout role.

Child anchor

Where a Drop to children originates.

For the Primary marriage of a multi-spouse person (and for any single-marriage Couple), the anchor is the midpoint of the Tie. For non-Primary marriages, the anchor is the bottom edge of the non-shared parent's box — the secondary spouse — so half-siblings hang directly under that spouse rather than under a long crossing Tie. For a rendered person with no spouse, the anchor is the bottom edge of their box.

Structures

Family

A nuclear unit: husband + wife + zero or more children.

One person can participate in several families — as a child in at most one, and as a spouse in zero or more. The going-up half of the chart hangs from the Family Focus is a child in; the going-down half hangs from the Family Focus is a spouse in.

Couple

A pair of spouses drawn side-by-side, joined by a Tie.

Couples appear wherever a married pair is rendered — ancestor pairs, the Focus with each of their spouses, and every rendered descendant with each of their spouses. Children of the Couple's Family hang from the Couple's Child anchor. An ancestor Couple with only one parent known degenerates to a single ancestor with no Tie.

Ancestor tree

The bloodline ancestry above a person, with lateral context at depth 1 only.

At depth 1: the bloodline Ancestor, that ancestor's full siblings (Aunts/Uncles), each non-Primary marriage as a Couple, and the non-bloodline children — Focus's Half-siblings at Focus row. At depth ≥ 2: the bloodline pair only. Recursion proceeds upward through the bloodline. Bounded by Generation limit.

Avoid: ancestor branch (it's a tree, not a single line), ancestor line.

Descendant tree

A person + each spouse + each child's Descendant tree, recursively.

Each rendered descendant appears with every one of their spouses (one Couple per spouse, each joined by its own Tie). Children of one Family hang from the corresponding Couple's Child anchor and are anchored by a Bar. Multifurcates downward by the number of children at each generation. Bounded by Generation limit.

Connectors

Tie

Horizontal segment between husband and wife at row-center Y.

Drawn between the inner edges of the two boxes inside any Couple, and inside the Focus row between Focus and Spouse. Sits at the row's centre Y; always horizontal.

Drop

Single vertical from the Child anchor down to the Bar.

Topmost piece of the drop / bar / leg trio. Originates at the Child anchor — the Tie midpoint of a Primary Couple, or the box bottom of a secondary spouse or a lone parent.

Bar

Horizontal line anchoring a sibship, sitting between two generations.

Middle piece of the trio. Spans from the first to the last sibling under one parent or Couple. Appears at every multi-child link in the chart, regardless of direction.

Leg

Short vertical from the Bar down to one sibling's box.

Bottom piece of the trio. One leg per sibling.

When there is only one rendered child, the Bar has zero width (collapses to a point), so the drop and leg meet directly. The drop+bar+leg structure still applies uniformly — no special-cased shortcut.

Vertical structure

Generation

A horizontal row, all boxes at the same Y coordinate.

The Focus row is generation 0. Generations above the focus row (parents, grandparents, …) are labeled 1↑, 2↑, …; generations below (children, grandchildren, …) are labeled 1↓, 2↓, …. All boxes on one Generation share the same Y.

Depth

Distance from the focus row at the current recursion step.

Always non-negative; direction (up vs down) is implicit in which side of the algorithm is running.

Generation limit

Maximum Depth rendered in each direction.

When the current Depth reaches the Generation limit (or no further family is known), recursion stops — the sub-layout becomes a single box with no further descent.

Viewport

Pin

Captured viewport coordinate that survives a focus change.

When the user clicks another person, the algorithm records that node's current screen position as a Pin. After re-layout, the canvas is panned so the newly-focused person reappears at the Pin's screen coordinate. Eliminates the "jump" effect on refocus.

Avoid: pan offset (that's the lower-level transform under the pin).

Layout primitives

Block

A positioned sub-region of the chart with a known horizontal extent and a local coordinate frame.

The recursive units (Ancestor tree, Couple, Descendant tree) are each a Block: a width, a Pivot, and the placed boxes and connectors inside. Blocks nest — a parent Block places children at offsets in its own local frame.

Pivot

The X coordinate at which a Block attaches upward.

For an Ancestor tree, the pivot is the X of the bottom person's box. For a Couple, the pivot is the midpoint of the Tie. For a multi-child Block, the pivot is the midpoint of the Bar.
